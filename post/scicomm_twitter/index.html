Workshop on social media for science communication

Sep 29, 2017

I’ve hesitated to commit to attending a full quarter of science communication classes, but I’ve been trying to take advantage of the half or 1-day workshops that the UW College of the Environment has been offering. This workshop was on using social media to communicate science, which means twitter just got a bunch of new accounts from UW scientists. The workshop was led by David Shiffman, who is currently at Simon Fraser University.

I learned a lot about the demographics of twitter (much older and more professional than I expected), and I was convinced that twitter can be a better platform for science-related content than other social media, particularly Facebook. So, I joined the darkside as @glaciologyDavid. We all puttered around for a while getting our bearings on the workings of twitter, posting gems like these:

Learning what this twitter thing is all about at the #ShiffmanWorkshop with other luddites like @OldAlienEarth.

— David A. Lilien (@GlaciologyDavid) September 29, 2017

I quickly became aware of just how well developed a tool this platform has become: apparently I’ve been oblivious to links on almost every journal article and news source that allow you to produce easy, nice-looking tweets of their content in a matter of seconds. Now if I only find some followers, I can spam them with everything I read!
